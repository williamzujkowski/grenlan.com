The Origin of Grenlan August 12, 2024

In the summer of '99, I was just getting into EverQuest, one of the pioneering massively multiplayer online role-playing games (MMORPGs). It was an era when dial-up internet was the standard, and the sound of a modem connecting was the prelude to countless adventures. EverQuest offered an expansive, immersive world called Norrath, where thousands of players could interact, embark on quests, and battle mythical creatures.

Choosing a character class was a pivotal decision in EverQuest, and I was drawn to the Bard. Bards were versatile adventurers known for their musical prowess, able to play enchanting songs that could boost allies' abilities, debuff enemies, and even travel faster across the vast landscapes. They were the jack-of-all-trades, bringing a unique blend of support and combat skills to any group.

Needing a name for my new half-elf bard, I faced the typical writer's block. That's when I remembered the Caesar cipher‚Äîa classic cryptographic technique where each letter in the plaintext is shifted a certain number of places down or up the alphabet. Picking numbers at random, I jumbled the letters of my name and created 'Grenlan'. It was unique, easy to remember, and perfectly suited for my bard's adventures in EverQuest. Check out my character profile.

Since then, Grenlan has been my unique identifier in many MMOs and games, from World of Warcraft to Dungeons and Dragons. It's a name that carries the spirit of adventure and camaraderie.

One highlight of my EverQuest journey is my high-end raiding guild, Triton. Sometimes, nostalgia hits, and I log in to join the raids, reconnecting with old friends and reliving epic battles. Those were the days of long, strategic encounters and the thrill of downing a tough boss after many attempts, all while hoping the connection wouldn't drop!
